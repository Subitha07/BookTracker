Rich Dad Poor Dad

Robert Kiyosaki

Rich Dad Poor Dad is a personal finance book by Robert Kiyosaki that tells the story of how his real father and his best friend's father shaped his views on money and investing. The book challenges the idea that a high income is required to become wealthy, and teaches readers how to manage their finances and make money work for them

Authors Here to Stay

Stephen King

The king of horror, Stephen King, has been enchanting readers for decades with his spine-chilling narratives. Stephen King’s “The Shining” remains his best-rated work, even after more than four decades of his prolific career. First published in 1977, “The Shining” has stood the test of time, appealing to multiple generations of readers.

Ulysses

James Joyce

Set in Dublin, the novel follows a day in the life of Leopold Bloom, an advertising salesman, as he navigates the city. The narrative, heavily influenced by Homer's Odyssey, explores themes of identity, heroism, and the complexities of everyday life. It is renowned for its stream-of-consciousness style and complex structure, making it a challenging but rewarding read.

In Search of Lost Time

Marcel Proust

This renowned novel is a sweeping exploration of memory, love, art, and the passage of time, told through the narrator's recollections of his childhood and experiences. The narrative is notable for its lengthy and intricate involuntary memory episodes. It explores the themes of time, space and memory, but also raises questions about the nature of art and literature
